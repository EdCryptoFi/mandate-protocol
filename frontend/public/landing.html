Core Properties

Autonomy without the blind spots.

Privacy and auditability. Autonomy and control. Mandate Protocol delivers both at once — positions stay private, decisions stay provable, and authority is enforced by the ledger, not by trust.

Authority in the ledger.

An allocation that exceeds the fund's mandate isn't flagged — it's rejected. Limits are cryptographic contracts the Canton protocol enforces at the transaction level.

Privacy fit for regulators.

Counterparties never see your full positions. Regulators see AI decisions and reasoning — not your balances. Canton's sub-transaction privacy enforces both, simultaneously.

Decisions recorded, forever.

Every call, allocation and distribution is stored immutably on-chain — what was done, why, and which limits were checked. The ledger is the audit trail and the LP report.

Authority Model

Delegation with control.

Three layers of authority. Each layer can only tighten the one above it. The AI agent operates exclusively at the bottom — and the Canton ledger rejects anything beyond its session.

I
CEO + CFO · two signatures required
Institution Mandate
The constitutional ceiling. Defines which assets, which counterparties, and the absolute maximum volumes. Cannot be exceeded by anyone — not even the CEO.
II
Compliance Officer + Treasury Manager
Operational Mandate
Day-to-day risk parameters: target allocations, rebalance thresholds, margin call limits. Can only tighten the Institution Mandate — never loosen it.
III
Treasury Manager
Agent Session
A time-limited token specifying exactly which actions are permitted today. Expires automatically. The AI agent operates exclusively within this layer.
AI
Agent · operates within the session only
Monitors the ledger, reasons with Claude, and submits actions — only within its session limits. If any assertion fails, the Canton ledger rejects the transaction. The ledger is the enforcer.
