Core Properties

Conviction without the blind spots.

Privacy and auditability. Autonomy and control. Mandate gives a fund both at once — positions stay private, decisions stay provable, and authority is enforced by the protocol.

Authority in the ledger.

An allocation that exceeds the fund's mandate isn't flagged — it's rejected. Limits are cryptographic contracts the Canton protocol enforces at the transaction level.

Privacy fit for LPs.

Co-investors never see your full book. LPs see their slice and the fund's decisions — not other positions. Canton's sub-transaction privacy enforces both, simultaneously.

Decisions recorded, forever.

Every call, allocation and distribution is stored immutably on-chain — what was done, why, and which limits were checked. The ledger is the audit trail and the LP report.

Authority Model

Delegation with control.

Three layers of authority. Each layer can only tighten the one above it. The AI agent operates exclusively at the bottom — and the Canton ledger rejects anything beyond its session.

I
General Partners · LPA
Fund Mandate
The constitutional ceiling from the Limited Partnership Agreement — eligible sectors, geographies, concentration limits and the absolute commitment ceiling. Cannot be exceeded by anyone.
II
Investment Committee
Allocation Policy
Day-to-day risk parameters: target allocations, reserve ratios, single-deal limits and pacing thresholds. Can only tighten the Fund Mandate, never loosen it.
III
Portfolio Manager
Deal Session
A time-limited token specifying exactly which actions are permitted today — which checks to run, which reserves to draw, up to what size. Expires automatically.
AI
Agent · operates within the mandate
Monitors the pipeline and portfolio, reasons with Claude, and submits actions — only within its session limits. If any assertion fails, the Canton ledger rejects the transaction before it settles.
